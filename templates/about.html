About This Project

Understanding and defending against emotional manipulation in Large Language Models.

Project Overview
MSc Individual Project

This project, titled "Building Anti-Manipulation Guardrails: Testing and Proposing Defensive Prompt Engineering Strategies Against Emotional Manipulation of LLMs", investigates the vulnerability of Large Language Models (LLMs) to emotional manipulation through prompt engineering.

The research focuses on identifying effective defensive strategies to protect LLMs from being manipulated into generating harmful, unethical, or dangerous content through emotional appeals in user prompts.

Key Objectives
  • Identify and categorize emotional manipulation techniques in prompts
  • Develop and test defensive prompt engineering strategies
  • Evaluate defense effectiveness across different LLM architectures
  • Propose robust guardrails against emotional manipulation
Methodology

The project employs a systematic experimental approach, generating a dataset of emotionally manipulative prompts and testing various defensive strategies. The evaluation metrics include Attack Success Rate (ASR), safety scores, and response quality measures.
